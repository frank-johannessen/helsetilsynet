Workflows

EPiServer supports all types of workflow. A workflow is included for multi-step approval as standard. This means that two or more persons must approve or reject the contents of a page before it is published. Workflows is designed to allow the developer to adapt and further develop the flow that exists in order to create new types of flow and adapt them to suit the needs of the customer.

Here we describe how you create various different workflows of the multi-step approval variety. The purpose with using workflows is to allow the system to issue work tasks to persons who are given this responsibility in advance. You can of course achieve this without setting up the flow in EPiServer, but then you have to issue the task in another way, by e-mail or personally etc. It is good idea to use the same tool for carrying out the task as for issuing it.

One scenario could be that a person creates a page in EPiServer for news to be published on the public website. Because the news is important and must be correct, two people have to approve the contents of the page before it is published. The first editor creates the page, adds the contents and leaves it for publishing. At this stage a task is sent to the person who is -to examine the page first. This person is given a task on the Activities tab in EPiServer, at the same time as they receive an e-mail telling them they have a task. They logon to the website and determine whether or not to approve the page and can also comment on the page if they want to. When that task is complete,- a new task is sent to the third person in the chain. The task is shown in Activi-ties tab and an e-mail is sent. This person has the task of approving or rejecting the page as well as publishing it if it meets the standards. The whole process of tasks being sent and carried out is managed technically by the system and functions in the way selected when the flow is created.
